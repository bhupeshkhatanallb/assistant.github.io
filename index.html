Full paper analysis — understand difficulty pattern for technical questions.

Very similar technical syllabus. Available on Testbook / Gradeup archives.
SSC CHSL/CGL Computer Awareness sections
Good for hardware, software, networking MCQs at the right difficulty level.
GATE CS previous year MCQ sections
Data Structures, DBMS, OS, Networks — pick objective questions only.
Negative marking: DHC uses −0.25 negative marking. Skip if you're less than 60% confident. Don't guess randomly.
C code trace questions: These appear heavily. Practice predicting output without running code. Focus on pointers, post/pre-increment, and logical operators.
DBMS normalization: Expect 3–5 questions on normal forms (1NF–BCNF). "Initial Normal Form" does NOT exist — always pick "First Normal Form" as the lowest level.
Port numbers to memorize: HTTP=80, HTTPS=443, FTP=21, SSH=22, Telnet=23, DNS=53, SMTP=25, POP3=110. These appear every year.
Number conversions: Practice decimal↔binary↔octal↔hex daily in Phase 1. 5 minutes/day builds speed. Use GeeksForGeeks practice MCQs.
Time strategy in exam: Technical (CS) questions first — they carry more marks. Then aptitude. Leave ambiguous GK for the end. Target: 45 min tech · 30 min aptitude · 15 min GK · 10 min review.
Current affairs shortcut: From 3 weeks before exam, read PIB.gov.in daily for 10 min. Focus on ISRO launches, govt schemes, awards — court exam favourites.
MS Office questions: Know MS Word shortcuts (Format Painter = Alt+Ctrl+C), PowerPoint (Ctrl+M = new slide), Excel basics. These give easy marks.

The one rule that will save you

1
Validate the Problem Do this now
Weeks 1–6 · Before writing a single line of code
▼
Do not skip this. You need lawyers to confirm — in their own words — that this pain is real and they will pay to fix it.
1.1
Share your survey aggressively
Send Jotform survey to every lawyer you know. Post in Delhi Bar Association WhatsApp groups. Post on LinkedIn tagging "Delhi High Court", "advocates", "LegalTech India". Target: 50+ responses in 2 weeks.
Do this week
1.2
Have 15 real conversations with lawyers
Not forms — real calls. Target junior advocates (25–32 yrs) at Delhi NCR chambers. Ask: "Walk me through the last time a filing was returned." Listen. Do NOT pitch yet.
Most important step
1.3
Visit DHC registry in person
Spend 2 days at the DHC filing counter. Watch filings being returned. Talk to registry staff. Observe which defects come up most. This is ground truth no survey can give you.
High value
1.4
Manually build a defects database
From your interviews and registry visits, create a spreadsheet of every defect type you encounter. Name, frequency, cause, how it could be detected automatically. This becomes the foundation of your rules engine.
Foundation
Answer these before Phase 2
Are filings returned frequently enough for lawyers to care?
Which 5–8 defect types cause 80% of all rejections at DHC?
Will junior advocates use this or will seniors block it?
Is ₹999/month realistic or does pricing need to change?
Do lawyers have data privacy concerns about uploading client documents?
2
Find a Legal Co-founder
Weeks 4–10 · Your single biggest risk right now
▼
The rules engine is only as good as your legal knowledge. You need a lawyer deeply involved — not a monthly advisor, a partner who lives this problem. Without this, your accuracy will be questioned.
2.1
Ideal co-founder profile
Practicing advocate, 3–8 yrs experience, has personally felt the pain of filing rejections, open to tech, files regularly at DHC. Not retired, not consulting — a working advocate with equity stake, excited about the problem.
Must have
2.2
Where to find them
Your Phase 1 lawyer interviews — look for the ones who got excited and asked follow-up questions. Post in LegalTech India communities on LinkedIn. Reach out via NLU alumni networks (NLUD, NLU Delhi grads have the most DHC exposure).
2.3
Fallback: law firm design partner
If no co-founder, partner with a small firm (3–8 lawyers) as a design partner — free lifetime access in exchange for rule-writing expertise, real test filings, and credibility.
Good fallback
Also do in this phase
Register Private Limited company via Razorpay Rize or Startups.gov.in (₹8K–15K)
Secure myfiling.ai domain — register NOW before someone else does
Decide legal disclaimer and terms of service with co-founder before any user touches the product
Decide data retention policy (delete PDFs after analysis? 24hrs? 30 days?)
Set up data privacy policy — DPDP Act 2023 compliance is mandatory
3
Build the MVP — DHC Only
Weeks 6–16 · Build the minimum. Ship fast. Improve after.
▼
Your MVP doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to be accurate enough to be useful and honest about what it cannot do. Ship it with a clear disclaimer.
3.1
Write the DHC rules engine first (no code yet)
Document every detectable rule in a spreadsheet: Rule ID, rule text, what the tool checks, defect title, severity (Critical/Minor/Warning), page/section. Start with 8–10 rules causing 80% of rejections. This spreadsheet IS your product logic.
Before coding
3.2
Build 2 screens only
Screen 1: PDF upload + court selection. Screen 2: defect results with severity + page references + probability score. That is it. Stack: Next.js frontend, Python FastAPI backend, pdfplumber for text PDFs.
Scope discipline
3.3
Test on 50 real filings before showing anyone
Get 50 actual DHC filings — mix of passed and returned. Run through your tool. Manually verify every output. Accuracy target before pilot: 85%+. Below that — do not pilot yet. Fix the gaps first.
Quality gate
3.4
Build the downloadable defect report
A PDF report a junior can hand to their senior. Include MyFiling.ai logo, court name, date, file name, clean defect list with page references. When a senior sees this report, they become aware of you — it's free word-of-mouth marketing built into the product.
Viral loop
3.5
Add login + 3 free analyses/month
Use Clerk or Supabase Auth. Free tier (3 analyses/month) from day one — no credit card required. Add Razorpay for paid subscriptions now so it's ready for launch.
Revenue ready
Do NOT build: bulk upload · team accounts · filing history · rule explanations · auto-corrections · multi-court support · API. Every feature added now delays launch by days.
4
Closed Pilot with Real Users
Weeks 16–22 · Most valuable 6 weeks of your journey
▼
Every bug you find here is a bullet dodged. Treat every negative feedback as a gift — it's free consulting from your most important customers.
4.1
Sign 3 pilot law firms (not individuals)
Firms give you volume — a single junior at a 10-person firm may run 15–20 analyses in 6 weeks. Offer 60-day free access in exchange for: weekly feedback calls, permission to use anonymised filing patterns to improve accuracy, and a testimonial if satisfied.
Key action
4.2
WhatsApp group with all pilot users
Create "MyFiling.ai Pilot Team" group. When they find a bug, they report in 10 seconds. Respond to every message within 2 hours. Lawyers talk to each other — how you handle the pilot group becomes your reputation.
Trust builder
4.3
Track accuracy obsessively
Ask pilot users to report every missed defect and every false positive. Track in a spreadsheet. Target: end pilot with 90%+ accuracy. Below that — do not launch publicly. Fix the gaps first.
Non-negotiable
4.4
Collect case studies, not just testimonials
"This tool is great" is useless. "We caught a missing vakalatnama in 3 seconds for a ₹2 crore dispute" is powerful. Get specific stories with permission to publish. These will do more for your launch than any ad.
Marketing gold
The one question to ask at end of pilot: "If we switched this off tomorrow, would you miss it?" If majority say yes — launch. If they say "useful but not essential" — more work to do.
5
Public Launch
Week 22–26 · Launch small, launch right
▼
Do not launch to everyone at once. Launch to the legal community in Delhi first — tight, word-of-mouth driven. A big Product Hunt launch with no lawyer audience = 500 sign-ups and zero paying customers.
5.1
Email your entire waitlist on Day 1
Every email from your Jotform survey gets a personal-feeling message from you. Not a newsletter blast — a short direct message: "You signed up 3 months ago. MyFiling.ai is live for Delhi HC. Here is your free access." Subject: "Your free MyFiling.ai access is ready, [Name]"
Day 1
5.2
LinkedIn launch post with a real case study
From your personal account — not the company page. Tell a real story from the pilot: "A junior caught a missing court fee error 10 minutes before filing a ₹50 lakh matter." Include a screenshot. Tag Delhi Bar Association. Human, specific, credible — beats any ad.
Day 1
5.3
Approach Delhi Bar Association for formal tie-up
Formal letter offering: free demo session for junior advocates, free access for DBA members for 30 days, co-branded "Filing Guide" for DHC. Bar association endorsement is worth more than any marketing spend.
Week 1–2
5.4
Partner with one law school
NLU Delhi, Faculty of Law DU, or Amity Law School. Free access for their legal aid clinic and moot court. Law students graduate and become junior advocates in 1–3 years. One partnership = 300–500 future users.
Long-term pipeline
5.5
Launch pricing: Free + Pro at ₹799/month
Start below target ₹999 as early-bird rate. Announce: "Launch pricing — ₹799/month for first 100 users, then ₹999." Monthly subscriptions only — no annual plans yet. Introduce annual plans once you have proven 3-month retention.
Pricing
6
Scale and Grow
Month 7–12 · Only after 30+ paying happy users
▼
Do NOT start this phase until you have at least 30 paying users who are actively using the product. Growing a broken or under-used product just accelerates failure.
6.1
Add Bombay High Court (Month 7–8)
BHC is the second-highest volume court in India — large, tech-forward legal community. Doubles your total addressable market. By this point you know the rules engine build process from DHC — BHC should take half the time.
Revenue multiplier
6.2
Launch the Firm plan (Month 8–9)
Team accounts, shared filing history, senior can see all filings by juniors. Moves you from individuals to firms — one sale, 5–10 seats. CAC stays the same but revenue per customer triples. Target firms with at least one active Pro user — upsell from bottom up.
Revenue leap
6.3
Build a referral programme
Lawyers trust lawyers. "Refer a colleague, get one month free." The downloadable defect report already creates passive referrals every time a junior sends it to their senior. Make that loop explicit and reward it.
Low cost, high return
6.4
Publish your accuracy data publicly
By Month 9 you'll have thousands of real filings analysed. Publish an anonymised accuracy report: "MyFiling.ai detected defects in X% of filings at DHC. Here are the most common errors." Drives organic search, LinkedIn shares, media coverage — all for free.
Credibility flywheel
6.5
Explore fundraising (Month 10–12)
With 50+ paying users, 90%+ accuracy, 2 courts covered — you have traction for investors. Target: iSPIRT, LetsVenture, Stellaris Venture Partners, Kalaari Capital. Use funding to hire a legal content manager and a second developer. Do NOT fundraise before you have real usage data.
